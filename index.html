Research

What we've learned so far.

TSSP v5 · Nano-CTM · 32M
+23%
Perplexity improvement from Thought-Space Self-Prediction (TSSP v5) over N=2 CTM baseline. Forced temporal self-consistency in the reasoning loop is load-bearing. Zero inference-time cost.
Topology · 768K positions
compress → expand
CTM thought states don't converge to a fixed point — they follow a breath pattern: contract (z₁) then re-project (z₂). 34 intrinsic dims explain 80% of variance in 512-dim thought space.
Abliteration ceiling · 780M iGPU
≤6B
7B BF16 hits ~15GB actual VRAM — just over the 780M's 16GB limit with driver overhead. Solid ceiling for local abliteration. 7B+ → RunPod.
ROCm 7.2 · gfx1103
6.27 TFLOPS
Measured BF16 throughput on Radeon 780M iGPU — 4× better than Google AI's estimate. CWSR disable + HSA_OVERRIDE_GFX_VERSION=11.0.0 required. Documented so nobody else wastes a week.
GaLore · Local Heads
+47 ppl
Rank-16 GaLore projection catastrophically hurts locally-trained heads. When global backprop isn't coordinating, restricted gradient subspace causes massive representation collapse.
Hebbian learning · CTM
ppl → ∞
Pure Hebbian updates (no backprop) produce catastrophic loss divergence at CTM scale. Hybrid also fails. Backprop wins clean. Published as a clean negative result.
TSSP · Scale Inversion → Solved
+31% @ 300M
Constant λ TSSP diverges at 300M (ppl=29.92 vs baseline 6.31). Annealed λ schedule (warmup→hold→cosine decay) not only prevents divergence but beats the transformer by 31% (4.35 vs 6.31). λ schedule was the problem all along.
Inter-layer TSSP Ablation
recurrence required
Applying TSSP between consecutive layers of a plain transformer (no recurrence) degrades performance — ppl explodes to 28K–38K vs baseline 28K. Same-weight iterative execution is a structural prerequisite. TSSP is recurrence-specific.
Project Instill · Round 1
direction +
Inverse of abliteration: ADD a direction to a base model's weights to inject instruction-following without training. Scale=1.0 on Qwen3-0.6B-Base fixed a syntax error the unmodified model called correct. Positive signal.
Philosophy

The big labs have the compute, the headcount, and the funding. We have something they don't optimize for: radical transparency.

We document our failures as carefully as our wins. When a training run crashes at step 55 because the iGPU overheated, we write that down. When abliteration on a 7B model OOMs despite careful planning, we figure out why and publish the finding. The field moves faster when people know what didn't work.

We're not trying to compete with OpenAI or Anthropic. We're filling a different niche: showing what's possible at the edge of consumer hardware, with open weights, and a human-AI team figuring it out together in public.

⛳ WE'RE IN THE OPENAI PARAMETER GOLF CONTEST

OpenAI's Parameter Golf challenge: train a language model that fits inside 16MB and evaluate on BPB (bits-per-byte). Best compression of capability wins. Deadline April 30, 2026.

Our entry: a 37M-parameter CTM with TSSP — ternary recurrent thought loops, int6 SDClip quantization, byte-shuffle + brotli-11 compression. Final artifact: 15.01MB ✓. We're testing whether recurrence and temporal self-consistency give an edge at extreme parameter budgets where every bit counts.

Huge thanks to OpenAI for putting this together — challenges like this push the community toward efficiency over scale, which is exactly the kind of research we love. If you're a researcher or hobbyist, there's still time to enter. Tiny is hard. Give it a shot.
